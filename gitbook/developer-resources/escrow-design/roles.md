# Roles

In **Trustless Work**, roles are at the heart of creating adaptable payment flows. By defining who holds each role, you can customize how transactions are managed, making the platform flexible enough to suit diverse use cases.

Whether you're building a marketplace, managing service agreements, or creating a platform for secure payments, understanding and strategically assigning roles is the foundation for success.

***

#### Key Roles and Their Impact

**1. Client (e.g., Alice) 👩**

**Purpose:** The party initiating the transaction by depositing funds into escrow. Often the buyer or the entity receiving services or goods. This role is also responsible for approving milestones, confirming that the product or service has been received and accepted.

**2. Service Provider (e.g., Bob) 👨🏻‍🦲**

**Purpose:** The party delivering the product or service. Responsible for marking milestones as completed, signaling the client to review and approve them.

**3. Platform (e.g., eBay) 🤖**

**Purpose:** An optional intermediary facilitating the transaction ecosystem. The platform acts as a neutral facilitator and may charge fees for providing a middleman service. It can also take on additional roles, such as dispute resolver or escrow manager.

**5. Release Signer (e.g., Alice) 🖊️**

**Purpose:** The individual or entity responsible for approving the release of funds after verifying that all conditions have been satisfied, including milestones being marked as approved.

**6. Dispute Resolver (e.g., Platform or Third Party)  👩🏽‍⚖️**

**Purpose:** The entity or mechanism that steps in if a dispute arises. This role ensures fairness by reviewing disputes, analyzing evidence, and determining the final allocation of funds.

**7. Receiver (e.g., Bob) 🤑**

**Purpose:** The final recipient of the funds after all conditions are met or disputes are resolved. Typically, the service provider or the seller.

***

#### Building Success with Roles in Trustless Work

By strategically assigning these roles, you can design payment flows that fit the needs of your platform or business. Whether you’re looking to balance control, increase trust, or streamline processes, the flexibility of Trustless Work allows you to create custom solutions that empower secure and transparent transactions.
